Investigation Overview¶

This investigation explores the differnce in bike usage by the two user types. Rides are conudcted by either subscribers or customers. Customers are riders with a 24-hour pass or 3-day pass while subscribers are riders with annual memberships. They are likely to be different groups with subscribers being residents that use the bikes consistently throughout the year. Customers are likely to be tourists or residents that only use the bikes sparsely. The investigation reveals key different usage patterns among the two groups.

Dataset Overview¶

The data is a combination of 12 months of data from 2018 of the NYC CitibikeNYC ride sharing program. The set consists of 17,548,339 rides and includes tripduration, starttime, usertype, birth year, gender, and other fields that were not explored.

Ride Duration Distribution by User Type¶

The two groups of users use the bikes for different purposes which could translate to different ride durations. Subscribers are likely to use the bikes for consistent pruposes such as commuting while customers which could include tourists could be using bikes for city exploration and other purposes. The distribution of ride durations among the two user types suggests very different usage purpose. Subscribers take shorter trips with a median of 10 and a concentrtion of rides below that mark. Customers have an almost evenly distributed ride duration between 10 and 30 minutes and a much higher median at 22 minutes. The interquartile range for subscribers is from 6 to 16 while that of the customers is from 13 to 27. Customer trip durations are longer and more varied.

Percentage of Rides During Week Days and Weekend by Hour of Day¶

Customers and Subscribers are different groups and are likely using the bikes for different purposes. Customer usage patterns suggest a heavy use for commuting. The peaks of the bimodal distribution during the week at 8:45 A.M and after 5 P.M and change of distribution during the weekend to a normal distribution with a round peak during the middle of the day support this.
